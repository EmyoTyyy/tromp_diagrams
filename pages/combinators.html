Combinator zoo

A combinator is a closed λ-term — no free variables. Every fold of computation can be built from a small handful of them. Smullyan named them after birds in To Mock a Mockingbird; we keep his names.

All these combinators are loaded as built-ins in the visualizer — type them by name (S, Y, theta…) and they expand to the diagrams above. The page Learn covers how SKI alone is enough to encode anything else.
